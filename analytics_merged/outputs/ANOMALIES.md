# Structural anomalies and tensions (not DApp-level)

Each entry is a **recurring pattern** observed across the strict sample. Use for thesis discussion, not as individual DApp callouts.

### ANO-GOV-01 — Decentralised label with company ownership
*Pillar: governance | Prevalence: 0.0% (0 DApps)*

**What:** Co-occurrence of maximum decentralisation score with corporate ownership—suggests definitional overlap between product marketing and control reality.

**So what:** Discuss legal wrappers, token distribution, and whether 'decentralised' refers to protocol vs operator.

### ANO-GOV-02 — Governance token type but team-controlled process
*Pillar: governance | Prevalence: 0.0% (0 DApps)*

**What:** Tokens classified as governance assets while formal governance remains team-steered.

**So what:** Useful tension for token design vs participation narratives; not an error but a structural pattern.

### ANO-GOV-03 — Centralised decentralisation score with on-chain token governance
*Pillar: governance | Prevalence: 1.47% (1 DApps)*

**What:** On-chain voting exists while overall decentralisation is labelled centralised—often upgradeability or off-chain execution.

**So what:** Interpret via timelocks, multisigs, and scope of on-chain votes.

### ANO-GOV-04 — DAO ownership without formal governance enum
*Pillar: governance | Prevalence: 0.0% (0 DApps)*

**What:** Ownership attributed to a DAO while governance type is recorded as none.

**So what:** May reflect data lag, informal governance, or classification edge cases.

### ANO-MKT-01 — High market cap per active wallet (top decile among measured)
*Pillar: market | Prevalence: 10.29% (7 DApps)*

**What:** Valuation per user in the top decile relative to the strict sample—signals speculative depth or thin active-user bases.

**So what:** Compare to category norms; avoid single-name attribution at thesis level.

### ANO-MKT-02 — Protocol TVL multiples above token market cap
*Pillar: market | Prevalence: 8.82% (6 DApps)*

**What:** TVL materially exceeds floated valuation—common for wrappers, staking, or measurement timing effects.

**So what:** Discuss leverage, double-counting, and snapshot mismatch between DeFi metrics and equity-like mcap.

### ANO-MKT-03 — Unfunded rows above funded median market cap
*Pillar: market | Prevalence: 29.41% (20 DApps)*

**What:** Share of zero-raised DApps whose market cap exceeds the median of funded peers in the same strict universe.

**So what:** Highlights retail-driven or organic listings vs VC-funded cohorts.

### ANO-ADP-01 — Centralised label with very high user counts
*Pillar: behaviour | Prevalence: 2.94% (2 DApps)*

**What:** Some of the most popular DApps by active wallets are labelled centralised — users flock to them regardless of governance model.

**So what:** Adoption is driven by product quality and network effects, not decentralisation claims. Challenges the assumption that users care about governance.

### ANO-PARA-01 — The decentralisation paradox at scale
*Pillar: governance | Prevalence: 86.76% (59 DApps)*

**What:** In the strict sample, 86.8% of DApps are NOT fully decentralised, despite being built on decentralised infrastructure.

**So what:** The blockchain layer is decentralised but the application layer remains centralised — raising the question of whether DApp decentralisation is achievable or even desired by builders.

### ANO-ENG-01 — Gaming vs DeFi engagement gap
*Pillar: ecosystem | Prevalence: 79.41% (54 DApps)*

**What:** Gaming attracts 12,670,611 users with low per-user value; DeFi processes $299.1B volume. The value-per-user gap exceeds 100x between these sectors.

**So what:** User counts alone are misleading as a success metric. A DApp with 1M gaming users may generate less economic activity than a DEX with 10K traders.

### ANO-CHAIN-01 — Multi-chain deployment correlates with higher valuation
*Pillar: ecosystem | Prevalence: 70.59% (48 DApps)*

**What:** 70.6% of the strict sample deploys multi-chain. Legacy analysis shows a 1.3x market cap premium for multi-chain DApps.

**So what:** Unclear whether multi-chain causes growth or is simply affordable only for already-successful projects. Survivorship bias may inflate the premium.
